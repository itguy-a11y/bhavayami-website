A Luminary in
Classical Dance

With over 25 years of dedication to the art of classical dance, Smt. Ambily Rajeesh has woven her passion into a vibrant tapestry as a teacher, performer, and choreographer. Her journey led her to establish Bhavayami, a prestigious institution that shines as a guiding light for aspiring dancers eager to hone their skills.

An SAAC-accredited artist in Kuchipudi, Smt. Ambily's accolades include the esteemed Sree Sankaracharya Award (2005) and the Kerala Nruthya Mayuri Puraskar (2020). Her academic pursuits are equally impressive, holding a postgraduate degree in English Literature and a Diploma in Kuchipudi from REVA University, Bangalore.

From the tender age of five, she trained under the watchful eye of Smt. Sudha Peethambaran, mastering Bharatanatyam, Kuchipudi, and Mohiniyattam. Her talent shone brightly in youth festivals, culminating in a state-level victory in Bharatanatyam in 2003.

Since 2014, she has deepened her understanding of Kuchipudi under Smt. Geetha Padmakumar, while continuing her Bharatanatyam studies with Smt. Kalakshethra Prameela Hariharan and Sri. Kalakshethra Hariharan. At Bhavayami, Smt. Ambily nurtures every student — ensuring the legacy of classical dance continues to flourish.

Bharatanatyam Classes in Kuwait

Bharatanatyam

Often described as the "fire dance," Bharatanatyam is one of the oldest and most popular classical styles. Rooted in the temples of South India, it teaches children discipline, focus, and grace through geometric precision and expressive storytelling — a living thread connecting your child to their Kerala heritage while living in Kuwait.

  • Adavus, Mudras & Abhinaya — expression and emotion
  • Classical compositions: Alarippu, Jatiswaram, Varnam & Tillana
  • The iconic Araimandi (half-sit) posture and geometric footwork
  • Carnatic music theory for a complete understanding of rhythm

Kuchipudi Classes in Kuwait

Kuchipudi

Kuchipudi is where dance becomes storytelling. Faster and more theatrical than Bharatanatyam, it blends pure rhythm with acting — making it especially engaging for young learners. Taught by Smt. Ambily Rajeesh, an SAAC-accredited Kuchipudi artist trained under legendary Gurus in Kerala, every class carries the authenticity of true Gurukula-style training.

  • Flowing movements and expressive Abhinaya
  • The iconic Tarangam — balancing on a brass plate with a pot of water
  • Yakshagana-style dramatic storytelling and presentation
  • The Kacham costume draping and its cultural significance
